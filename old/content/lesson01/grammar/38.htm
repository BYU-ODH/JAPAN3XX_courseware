V よう(おう）とする:
try to V

Explanation: This pattern expresses the intention or the volition of the speaker to do the verb. Note that the bases are different for the ichidan and godan verbs. Ichidan verbs take "yoo (よう)" after the verb stem while the godan verbs take "oo (Base V + う)" after the stem. In both cases the accent drops before the final う in standard Japanese.

例1. 立とうとしたが足がしびれて立てなかった。
He tried to stand up, but his legs were asleep so he couldn't.

例2. 戸を開けようとしてこわしてしまった。
Trying to open the door, he broke it.
