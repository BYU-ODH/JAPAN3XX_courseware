おのづからみじかき運を悟りぬ
おのづからみじかきうんをさとりぬ

Definition: The ぬ ending in classical Japanese can also show a definite completion of the verb. For example, the title of the famous book and movie "Gone with the Wind" in Japanese is 風と共に去りぬ（かぜとともにさりぬ）. The ぬ on the end of「去りぬ」is like the「…てしまった」or the「た」form in modern Japanese「去ってしまった」or「去った」, and implies that the verb was utterly and completely finished: "Gone (utterly and completely, never to return again) with the Wind.”
