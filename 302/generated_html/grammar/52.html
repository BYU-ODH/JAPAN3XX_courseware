〜よ:
emphasis;
exclamatory

Explanation: By adding よ at the end of a sentence, the speaker is claiming that the information presented in the sentence is his own information. Hence, make sure that the statement is something that you can claim, otherwise it could sound overconfident and convey arrogance or overfamiliarity.

例1. がまんしていればよくなりますよ。
If you put up with it, you'll get better.

例2. 借金でもしないとやっていけないんですよ。
We can't get by without going into debt.
