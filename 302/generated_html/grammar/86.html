N と言われる(ている・た):
called to be N

Explanation: The literal translation is "called to be N," but this phrase is used to introduce N as something very well-known by the people: "it is said" or "called N."

例1. 特に、維新の三傑と言われた西郷が西南戦争で死に、木戸が病死し、大久保が暗殺された。
In particular, the so-called three heroes of the Meiji restoration died: Saigō in the Seinan Conflict, Kido because of an illness, and Ōkubo was assassinated.

例2. 発明の父と言われるエジソンは、アメリカのオハイオ州で生まれた。
Edison, the "Father of Invention," was born in Ohio.

例3. 紫式部は、世界的女流作家と言われている。
Lady Murasaki is known as a world-famous female writer.
