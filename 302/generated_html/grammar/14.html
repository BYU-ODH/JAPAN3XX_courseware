N に:
N can;
as for N (potential or existence)

Explanation: This structure is called “dative に.” When the predicate indicates “potential” or “existence,” the noun that can do or exists can be marked with に.

例1. あの人にラテン語が読めるわけがない。
There is no way he can read Latin.

例2. わたしにはよくわかりません。
I don't know that much about it (I can't understand it well.)
