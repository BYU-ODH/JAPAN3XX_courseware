Nと言えば：
Speaking of N

Explanation: This sentence structure is often used to introduce the topic of the conversation, or sometimes to change the course of the conversation to focus on N. In other words, the speaker introduces N so that he/she can talk about N.

例1. アメリカで、ジョージ・ワシントンとか、トーマス・ジェファーソンと言えば、独立のために貢献した人として、知らない人はほとんどいないでしょう。
In America, few people would not know people such as George Washington or Thomas Jefferson to be men who contributed to the independence of the country.

例2. 伊藤博文と言えば、大日本帝国憲法の草案者として知らない日本人はいないはずだ。
Few Japanese would not know the name of Hirobumi Itō as a man who drafted the Great Imperial Japanese Constitution.

例3. 日本と言えば、アメリカ人は何を連想するでしょう。
When you say Japan, what do Americans think of first?
